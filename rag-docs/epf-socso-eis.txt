Managing employees in Malaysia requires registration with three statutory bodies: EPF, SOCSO, and EIS. These contributions are mandatory for all Malaysian employees (and some for foreigners).
1. Contribution Rate Tables
Rates are based on the employee's monthly wages (Salary + Allowances).
A. EPF (Employees Provident Fund / KWSP)
Calculated based on the Third Schedule of the EPF Act 1991.

| Employee Age | Monthly Wages | Employee Rate | Employer Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Below 60 | RM5,000 & below | 11% | 13% |
| Below 60 | Above RM5,000 | 11% | 12% |
| 60 - 75 | Any amount | 0%* | 4% |

*Note: Employees aged 60+ can choose to maintain their 11% contribution, but the statutory default is 0%.
B. SOCSO (Social Security Organization / PERKESO)
Employment Injury & Invalidity Scheme.

| Category | Employee Rate | Employer Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Standard (Below 60) | ~0.5% | ~1.75% |
| 60+ / New Joiners 55+ | 0% | ~1.25% (Injury Scheme only) |

SOCSO uses a Contribution Salary Ceiling of RM6,000 (effective Oct 2024). Even if an employee earns RM10,000, the contribution is capped at the RM6,000 rate.
C. EIS (Employment Insurance System / SIP)
Protects employees in case of retrenchment.

| Wage Level | Employee Rate | Employer Rate |
|---|---|---|
| All Employees | 0.2% | 0.2% |

Like SOCSO, EIS is also capped at a RM6,000 salary ceiling.

2. Employer Registration Checklist
You must register as an employer the moment you hire your first employee.
EPF Registration (KWSP)

* Action: Register via KWSP i-Akaun (Employer).
* Documents Needed:
* Section 17/Certificate of Incorporation (SSM Form D or Form 9).
   * Identification: Copy of Director’s/Owner’s MyKad.
   * Form KWSP 1: Employer Registration Form.
* Deadline: Within 7 days of hiring the first employee.

SOCSO & EIS Registration (PERKESO)

* Action: Register via the PERKESO ASSIST Portal.
* Documents Needed:
* Employer Registration Form (Form 1).
   * Employee Registration Form (Form 2).
   * Business Profile: SSM Form D/Form 9.
   * Director MyKad: Copy of the authorized person's ID.
* Deadline: Within 30 days of hiring the first employee.

------------------------------
3. Monthly Payment Deadlines
Failure to pay on time results in Late Payment Interest (LPI) and fines.

* EPF: By the 15th of the following month.
* SOCSO/EIS: By the 15th of the following month.

4. Summary for SMEs

* Wages include: Basic salary, overtime, commissions, paid leave, and most allowances.
* Wages exclude: Service charges (tips), traveling allowances, and retirement gratuities.
* Foreign Workers: EPF is optional but SOCSO is mandatory (Employer pays 1.25% for Employment Injury Scheme; Employee pays 0%).
