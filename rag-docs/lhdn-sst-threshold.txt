Here are the detailed tax obligations for businesses in Malaysia under the Inland Revenue Board (LHDN) and Royal Malaysian Customs Department (RMCD).
1. Sales and Service Tax (SST) Registration
The SST is a single-stage tax levied on specific goods and services. Registration is mandatory when the total value of taxable goods or services for a 12-month period exceeds the prescribed threshold.

* Standard Threshold: RM500,000 annual turnover [(BoardRoom, 2025)](https://www.boardroomlimited.com/my/2025/06/17/malaysias-expanded-sales-and-services-tax-sst-takes-effect-on-1-july-2025/).
* Service Tax Rate: Increased from 6% to 8% effective 1 March 2024 (excluding F&B, telecommunications, and logistics which remain at 6%) [(HSBC Business Go, 2025)](https://www.businessgo.hsbc.com/en/article/sales-and-service-tax-sst-in-malaysia-2024).
* Specific Thresholds by Industry:
* F&B Operators: RM1,500,000 [(PwC, n.d.)](https://www.pwc.com/my/en/publications/mtb/service-tax.html).
   * Construction Work: RM1,500,000.
   * Private Healthcare & Vet Services: RM1,500,000.
   * Credit Card / Charge Card: No threshold (Registration is mandatory upon operation).
   * Customs Agents: No threshold.
* Registration Portal: Register online via the MySST Portal.

2. CP204 Tax Installment Payment Schedule [1] 
The CP204 is the estimate of tax payable for Companies, Limited Liability Partnerships (LLP), Trust Bodies, and Co-operatives. [1] 

* Who Must File: Mandatory for Sdn Bhd, LLPs, and Co-ops. Sole proprietorships and partnerships generally do not file CP204 (they pay via CP500).
* Filing Deadline:
* New Business: Must submit within 3 months of commencing operations [(Lembaga Hasil Dalam Negeri Malaysia, 2024)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/company/tax-estimation/).
   * Existing Business: Must submit 30 days before the beginning of the basis period for the Year of Assessment (YA) [(Grof, 2025)](https://grof.co/my/blog/lhdn-e-filing-deadline-2025-6-key-dates-for-sdn-bhd).
* Installment Schedule:
* Tax is paid in equal monthly installments.
   * Start Date: Payments typically begin in the 6th month of the basis period for new companies, or the 2nd month for existing companies (transitional rules may apply) [(KTP & Company PLT, 2025)](https://www.ktp.com.my/blog/cp-204-tax-instalment-payment/6nov2025).
   * Payment Deadline: By the 15th day of each month.
* Underestimation Penalty: If the final tax payable exceeds the estimated tax (CP204) by more than 30%, a penalty of 10% is imposed on the difference [(Singapore Corporate Services, 2025)](https://scs-cpa.com/corporate-income-tax-malaysia-rates-filing-and-strategic-compliance/). [2, 3, 4, 5] 

3. Employer Obligations: Form E & Form EA
Employers are responsible for reporting employee remuneration to LHDN annually.

* Form E (Employer's Return):
* Purpose: Declares the total number of employees and list of employee income details to LHDN.
   * Submission Deadline: 31 March of the following year [(Lembaga Hasil Dalam Negeri Malaysia, 2026)](https://www.hasil.gov.my/en/employers/).
   * Mandatory e-Filing: Must be submitted via MyTax Portal.
   * Failure to Submit: Offense under the Income Tax Act 1967, liable to fines or imprisonment.
* Form EA (Employee's Statement of Remuneration):
* Purpose: A statement provided to employees detailing their annual salary, bonuses, benefits-in-kind (BIK), and tax deductions.
   * Distribution Deadline: Must be given to employees by 28 February of the following year (Grof, 2025).
   * Required Details: Gross salary, PCB deducted, EPF/SOCSO contributions, and exempt allowances. [3, 6, 7, 8] 

4. PCB (Monthly Tax Deduction) Rates & Threshold [9] 
PCB (Potongan Cukai Berjadual) is a mechanism where employers deduct tax from employee salaries monthly. [10] 

* Liability Threshold:
* PCB deduction is mandatory if an employee's annual remuneration (after EPF deduction) exceeds RM34,000 (approx. RM2,833/month) [(AJobThing, 2025)](https://www.ajobthing.com/resources/blog/pcb-deduction-malaysia-guide).
   * For married employees with a non-working spouse, the threshold is higher (approx. RM3,851/month).
* Calculation Method:
* Computerised Calculation (Recommended): Use the official [LHDN PCB Calculator](http://calcpcb.hasil.gov.my/) or payroll software (e.g., SQL Payroll, Kakitangan) to ensure accuracy.
   * Manual Table (Reference): While LHDN provides a schedule, exact rates depend on category (Single/Married/Disabled) and number of children.
* Payment Deadline: Deducted amounts must be remitted to LHDN by the 15th day of the following month (Lembaga Hasil Dalam Negeri Malaysia, 2026). [9]
