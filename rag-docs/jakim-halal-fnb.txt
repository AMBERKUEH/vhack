For F&B businesses in Malaysia, JAKIM Halal Certification is the gold standard for consumer trust. The process is rigorous and governed by the Manual Procedure for Malaysia Halal Certification (MPPHM).


1. Scope of Certification
JAKIM (Department of Islamic Development Malaysia) or State Islamic Religious Departments (JAIN/MAIN) certify the following F&B categories:
Food Premise: Restaurants, Cafeterias, Food Courts, Hotel Kitchens.
Food Product: Food & Beverage manufacturing/factories.
Slaughterhouse: Meat and poultry processing.
2. Comprehensive Application Checklist
A. Legal & Administrative Documentation
SSM Registration: Valid Business Registration (Sole Prop/Partnership/Sdn Bhd).
Business License: Valid premise and signage license from Local Authorities (PBT).
Cleanliness Grade: Premise must achieve at least Grade A or B in Local Council inspections.
Financial Records: Proof of business operation (not required for new start-ups).
B. Human Resources Requirements
Halal Supervisor: At least two (2) permanent Muslim Malaysian employees in the kitchen/production section.
Training Records:
Halal Awareness Training: For all staff handling food.
Halal Competency Training: Specifically for the Halal Supervisor.
Note: Training must be conducted by JAKIM-recognized providers.
Health Compliance: All food handlers must have Typhoid Vaccination (Suntikan Thypoid) and a valid Health Certificate.
C. Material & Ingredient Control (Crucial)
Master List of Ingredients: A complete list of all raw materials, additives, and processing aids.
Halal Certification: Every ingredient must have a valid Halal Certificate from:
JAKIM.
JAIN/MAIN (State bodies).
JAKIM-Recognized Foreign Halal Bodies (e.g., MUIS Singapore, HMC UK).
Product Specification: For "high-risk" ingredients (flavors, enzymes, gelatins), a technical data sheet (Product Spec) is required to trace the source.
D. Operational Compliance
Halal Assurance System (HAS): Written Standard Operating Procedures (SOP) for:
Purchasing/Sourcing.
Receiving and Storage (separation of Halal vs. non-Halal).
Production/Preparation.
Zero-Alcohol/Pork Policy: No alcoholic beverages or non-halal meat allowed on the premise for any reason (including staff personal food).
Dedicated Equipment: Tools, utensils, and logistics (delivery vehicles) must be dedicated to Halal use only.
3. Renewal Cycle Information
Validity Period: Two (2) years for most F&B premises and products.
The "6-Month Rule": Renewal applications must be submitted via MYeHALAL at least six (6) months before the expiry date.
Late Renewal Consequence: If the application is submitted less than three (3) months before expiry, it is treated as a New Application, requiring a full, fresh audit and higher scrutiny.
Lapsed Certificate: If the certificate expires, the Halal logo must be removed/covered immediately from all packaging and signage to avoid heavy fines for "False Representation."
4. Fee Structure (Per Premise/Certificate)
Business Category	Estimated Fee (per 2 years)
Micro Industry (<RM300k turnover)	RM100.00
Small Industry (RM300k – RM15mil)	RM400.00
Medium Industry (RM15mil – RM50mil)	RM800.00
Food Premise (Hotel/Catering)	RM200.00 - RM1,000.00 (varies by star rating/size)
5. Common "Reject" Reasons 
Incomplete Ingredient Certificates: Using an expired Halal cert for a minor spice.
Staffing: Losing one of the two mandatory Muslim staff and not replacing them within the grace period.
Hygiene: Pest infestation (cockroaches/rodents) found during an unannounced site audit.
Naming: Product names that reference non-halal items (e.g., "Root Beer," "Ham Pizza," or "Turkey Bacon")—JAKIM generally requires neutral naming.

The registration process for JAKIM Halal certification is a multi-stage procedure managed primarily through the online MYeHALAL system. For a business to be eligible, it must be fully operational for at least three months to demonstrate its ability to sustain halal compliance. 

Step-by-Step Registration Process
Account Registration:
Create an account on the MYeHALAL Portal.
Verification of the account typically occurs within 24 working hours.
Online Application Submission:
Log in and select the relevant scheme (e.g., Food Premise for restaurants or Food Product for manufacturers).
Fill in all details accurately, including the list of products/menus and every ingredient used.
Document Upload:
Submit all supporting documents within five (5) working days of the online submission.
Key documents include:
SSM Registration and a valid Local Authority Business License.
Halal Certificates for every raw material/ingredient from JAKIM or recognized foreign bodies.
Production Flow Charts and factory/premise layout plans.
Employment contracts and ICs for at least two (2) permanent Muslim Malaysian workers.
Fee Payment:
Once the application is vetted for completeness, JAKIM will issue a Charges Letter.
Fees (processing and certification) must be paid within 14 working days of receiving this letter.
On-Site Audit & Inspection:
After payment, auditors will schedule an inspection.
They will verify ingredient sources, check for potential cross-contamination, and assess hygiene standards.
Auditors may take product samples for laboratory analysis to check for traces of alcohol or non-halal DNA.
Corrective Actions (If Necessary):
If any Non-Conformance Reports (NCR) are issued during the audit, the business must rectify the issues and provide proof of resolution within a specified timeframe.
Final Approval & Certification:
The audit report is reviewed by the Halal Certification Panel for a final decision.
Successful applicants will receive their Malaysia Halal Certificate, which is valid for two (2) years. 

Application Timeline
Total Duration: Typically ranges from 3 to 6 months, depending on document readiness and the speed of corrective actions.
Renewal: Must be initiated at least three (3) to six (6) months before the current certificate expires.
